A tree in computer science is one of the most fundamental and widely used data structures for organizing information hierarchically. It consists of nodes connected by edges where the topmost node is called the root and nodes with no children are called leaves. Trees are used extensively in databases artificial intelligence and operating systems.

The book you are reading right now is designed to take you from basic concepts all the way to advanced tree algorithms step by step. Each chapter builds on the previous one ensuring that the reader develops a thorough understanding of how trees work in practice. Diagrams code snippets and exercises are included throughout to reinforce learning.

This material is ideal for a newbie who has little or no prior experience with data structures. The language used is simple and approachable making even the most complex topics digestible for beginners. Whether you are a student a self-learner or a professional looking to upskill this book will serve as a valuable reference.
